The Trace tab, as a chain of custody

Every AI edit to a deck leaves a receipt. This panel renders one AgentTrace as six sealed nodes on a provenance rail — who authorized, what the agent read, planned and wrote, and the human-owned proof that binds it. A degraded link can never hide.

The rail leads the eye down to one loud terminal object: the Receipt node opens onto a tri-signature seal — Agent (model + candidate digest) countersigned by the Validator (ok · publish · clean) and awaiting the Human.

Switch traces to compare a live GLM 5.2 edit against a deterministic fallback where the model route timed out. Watch the rail break, honestly, at the exact hop it degraded — and the seal downgrade to a dashed-amber provisional stamp.

Filled diamond — human-owned hop (consent · validate · receipt)
Hollow diamond — agent-owned hop (read · plan · edits)
Dashed amber break — attribution degraded at this hop

Frame vocabulary adapted from Agent Prism by Evil Martians (MIT) — Oklch semantic tokens, typed badge+avatar per event, tabular mono numerics, row→attribute-table detail. The terminal Countersigned seal is grafted as the Receipt node's proof. Re-anchored to NodeSlide's terracotta/indigo inks; the OTEL span-tree waterfall is rejected in favor of a fixed six-node custody spine.
